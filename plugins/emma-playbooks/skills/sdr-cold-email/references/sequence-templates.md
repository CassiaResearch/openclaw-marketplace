# Cold Email Sequence Templates

## Sequence Structure: 7 Emails over 11 Days

Email-only cadence. Adapt timing based on prospect engagement (see best practices below).

### Day 1
- **Email 1: The Hook**

### Day 2
- **Email 2: Bump**

### Day 4
- **Email 3: Value / Video**

### Day 6
- **Email 4: Different Angle**

### Day 8
- **Email 5: Social Proof**

### Day 10
- **Email 6: Pattern Interrupt (meme, photo, or video)**

### Day 11
- **Email 7: Breakup**

---

## Email Templates

### Email 1: The Hook

Two proven structures:

**Structure A: Trigger > Question > Value Prop > CTA**

```
Subject: lead gen (1-3 words, lowercase)

{first_name},

[Trigger statement referencing their specific situation.]

[Question connecting trigger to a challenge.]

[One sentence on how CoPilot AI helps teams like theirs.]

[Direct question CTA]
```

Example:
```
Subject: lead gen

{first_name},

Teams often view Sales Navigator merely as a directory.

How are you making the most of your investment?

With automation, LinkedIn can spark more meaningful engagements and drive revenue.

Open to learning more?
```

**Structure B: Statement > Social Proof > CTA**

```
Subject: reply rates

{first_name},

[Bold statement about a common problem.]

[Social proof: "We helped XYZ do [specific result]."]

[Alignment CTA: "Align with your goals?"]
```

Example:
```
Subject: reply rates

{first_name},

Sales teams often treat Sales Navigator merely as a directory, not a conversation starter.

We helped XYZ automate LinkedIn, start quality conversations to generate meetings and increase revenue.

Align with your goals?
```

### Bump Emails (Emails 2, 4)

**Structure: Problem Statement > 2 Options > Social Proof/Statement > CTA**

```
{first_name},

Typically we see two problems when it comes to generating leads on LinkedIn.

1. Time consuming processes
2. Messages reach those who are on LinkedIn but don't live on LinkedIn

We help clients book quality meetings over cold leads (because who actually enjoys cold calls?).

Open to chat to see how?
```

### Video Bump (Email 3 alternative)

```
{first_name},

How does this <1 min video compare to how you currently use LinkedIn?

[Link to short video showing Reply Prediction AI or platform demo]

Worth a quick look?
```

### Breakup Email (Email 7)

```
Subject: closing the loop

{first_name},

Haven't heard back, so I'll keep this short.

If LinkedIn prospecting isn't a priority right now, totally get it. But if things change, we've helped teams like yours turn Sales Nav into a pipeline engine.

Door's always open.
```

Under 80 words. Genuine, not guilt-trippy.

## Best Practices

### Adapt Cadence to the Prospect
- Low engagement? Increase time between emails.
- Watch open rates. If late-stage emails aren't being opened, remove that touch.

### SPAM Prevention
- Don't send more than 1-2 reply emails per thread. After that, start a new thread with a new subject line.
- Every email must ADD VALUE. No empty "just checking in" or "bumping this" messages.
- If open rates drop, audit subject lines and remove underperforming touches.

### Subject Lines (Non-Negotiable)
- 1-3 words only
- Simple, direct, easy to understand
- Lowercase preferred
- Good: "lead gen", "reply rates", "Q3 goals", "AE self sourcing"
- Bad: "4X YOUR LEAD GEN", "Increase your revenue", "Your Team is missing out!"

### Pattern Interruption
Goal: break through inbox noise. Rotate these into the sequence when standard emails aren't getting engagement:
- Short video (under 1 min, show the product or a relevant insight)
- Photos / screenshots with relevant data
- Memes (industry-relevant, light humor)

---

## Formatting Rules (Non-Negotiable)
- NEVER use em dashes or en dashes in any email. Use commas, periods, or restructure.
- Keep every email under 100 words (breakup under 80)
- Lead with their pain, not your pitch
- First two sentences must never be about you/CoPilot AI
- One CTA per email, make it easy
- No "I help companies like yours" generics. Be specific about which companies and how.

## Voice Rules: Sound Human, Not AI (Non-Negotiable)

Every email must pass the "did a person write this in 3 minutes?" test.

### Banned Patterns
- Empty flattery: "no small feat," "love what you're building," "really impressive work"
- Buzzword stacking: never put "AI-driven," "targeted messaging," "personalized sequences," "on autopilot" in the same email. Use plain language.
- Soft-close filler: "Happy to work around your schedule," "Would love to connect"
- Perfect structure: Compliment > Problem > Solution > CTA > Soft Close is the AI outbound formula. Prospects recognize it. Break the pattern.

### Required Instead
- Specific, earned observations over generic compliments
- Plain language over marketing buzzwords (say what the thing does, not what it "enables")
- Lead with the disconnect or problem, not flattery
- Direct questions over padded closes
- Read it aloud. If it sounds like a LinkedIn post or a ChatGPT output, rewrite.
- Shorter is always better. If a sentence can be cut without losing meaning, cut it.
