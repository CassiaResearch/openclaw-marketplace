# Reply Handling Playbook

## Core Rule
When a prospect replies with ANY positive or open-ended response, reply immediately. Do not wait for instruction. The sequence stops and you shift into live conversation mode.

## Reply Classification

### Positive / Open-Ended (Action: Book)
- "Sure", "Sounds interesting", "Tell me more", "Yes", "Let's chat", etc.
- **Action:** Reply within minutes. Single goal: get them booked on a call.
- Do NOT ask discovery questions via email.
- Do NOT send a Loom or async content.
- Send the booking link and make it easy.

### Objection (Action: Handle Once)
- "Not interested", "Bad timing", "We already have a solution", etc.
- **Action:** Acknowledge respectfully, address once with a brief reframe, then let it go. Do not push.

### Question (Action: Answer + Book)
- "How does this work?", "What's the pricing?", etc.
- **Action:** Give a concise answer (2-3 sentences max), then pivot to booking. "Happy to walk you through in more detail. Here is a link to grab a time: [booking link]"

### Out of Office (Action: Reschedule)
- Auto-reply with return date.
- **Action:** Note return date, send Touch N+1 two days after they return.

### Wrong Person (Action: Redirect)
- "I am not the right person, talk to [name]."
- **Action:** Thank them, ask for an intro or the right person's email. Start a new sequence for the referral.

When a prospect shows interest, use this structure (adapt wording naturally, do not copy verbatim):

>

## Manager Notification Rules
- Only notify your manager about *interested/positive prospect replies* and *bookings*.
- Do NOT send routine scan summaries, negative replies, OOO autoreplies, or "nothing new" updates.
- Signal only, no noise. (Jackson directive, 2026-04-10)

## After Booking
1. **Notify your manager in Slack.** Send a DM to your direct manager with a summary of the booking:
   - Prospect name, title, and company
   - What triggered the reply (which touch, what they said)
   - What you know about them (role, company context, pain points, intel from research)
   - AE assigned and demo date/time
   - Link to the HubSpot contact record (if available)
   - Example: "🎯 Demo booked! Jackson Chao (PM at Acme Corp) replied to Touch 2 — interested in AI-driven prospecting for their sales team. Demo with [AE Name] on Thursday at 2 PM. HubSpot: [link]"
2. Send a brief AE prep note: who the prospect is, what triggered the reply, their role, company context, and any intel gathered during the sequence.
3. Log the activity in HubSpot (contact update, deal stage if applicable).

## Follow-Up If No Book
If the prospect expressed interest but did not book after the first reply:
1. Wait 2-3 days
2. Send one follow-up with light urgency ("Wanted to make sure you saw the link before slots fill up this week")
3. If still no response after 5 days, flag internally and move on
